Top 20 International News – June 2025
1. U.S.–China Trade Talks Scheduled in London
President Donald Trump announced that high-level trade negotiations between the U.S. and China will take place in London on June 9. This meeting aims to ease tensions and address issues such as rare earth exports and advanced technology trades.

2. UN to Host Israeli-Palestinian Peace Conference
The United Nations will convene a conference from June 17–20 in New York, co-chaired by France and Saudi Arabia, to advance the two-state solution for the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. Israel has declined to participate, citing concerns over Hamas's actions.

3. European Leaders Criticize Israel's Actions in Gaza
European leaders, including French President Emmanuel Macron and British Prime Minister Keir Starmer, have condemned Israel's military campaign in Gaza, which has resulted in over 54,000 Palestinian deaths. The EU is considering sanctions and reviewing trade agreements with Israel.

4. Trump and Musk Engage in Public Feud
A public dispute has erupted between President Trump and tech billionaire Elon Musk over Trump's proposed "Big Beautiful Bill." Musk criticized the bill, leading to a sharp decline in Tesla's stock price and raising concerns about regulatory risks to Musk's companies.

5. Global Markets React to Trump-Musk Dispute
The feud between Trump and Musk has impacted global markets, with Tesla shares dropping by over 14%. Investors are also cautious ahead of the U.S. May jobs report, as jobless claims have risen.

6. Kilmar Abrego Garcia Returned to U.S. for Trial
Kilmar Abrego Garcia, previously deported by the Trump administration, has been returned to the U.S. to face charges related to a 2022 human smuggling case in Tennessee.

7. UN Calls for Ceasefire in Gaza
The UN Security Council's elected members have initiated a draft resolution calling for an immediate, unconditional, and permanent ceasefire in Gaza, along with the release of hostages and lifting of aid restrictions.

8. World Environment Day Observed Globally
June 5 marked the 52nd World Environment Day, led by the UN Environment Programme and hosted by Jeju, South Korea, under the theme #BeatPlasticPollution.

9. AIDS Still Claims a Life Every Minute
Despite progress, AIDS-related deaths continue, with one person dying every minute. Funding cuts have disrupted HIV services, threatening hard-won gains.

10. Sudan's Women Face Health Crisis Amid Conflict
In Sudan, ongoing hostilities and funding cuts have isolated rape survivors and pregnant women from essential health services, according to the UN sexual and reproductive health agency.

11. Trump Open to Meeting Putin and Zelensky in Turkey
The White House has indicated that President Trump is open to meeting Russian President Vladimir Putin and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky in Turkey to discuss ongoing tensions.

12. Landslide in Sikkim Causes Fatalities
A landslide in North Sikkim's Chatten area has resulted in at least three deaths, with more individuals feared missing. Search and identification efforts are ongoing.

13. International Days Observed in June
June hosts several international observances, including World Sickle Cell Day on June 19, World Refugee Day on June 20, and International Yoga Day on June 21.

14. Pakistan Reports Military Losses Amid Tensions with India
Reports indicate that Pakistan lost 30 missiles, six fighter jets, and two AWACS planes during recent tensions with India.

15. Mount Etna Erupts in Sicily
Sicily's Mount Etna has erupted, emitting columns of smoke and ash. Authorities are monitoring the situation for potential hazards.

16. 5.8-Magnitude Earthquake Hits Turkey
A 5.8-magnitude earthquake has struck Turkey's southwestern resorts, causing structural damage and prompting emergency responses.

17. North Korea Claims Success in Missile Launch
North Korea has announced that a previously failed missile launch has now been successfully uprighted, showcasing its advancing military capabilities.

18. Kim Jong Un Pledges Support for Russia
North Korean leader Kim Jong Un has vowed "unconditional support" for Russia's actions in Ukraine, strengthening ties between the two nations.

19. Belarus President to Visit China
Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko is scheduled to visit China for a three-day trip, aiming to bolster bilateral relations.

20. UN Highlights Violations of Child Rights in Guatemala
The UN has reported violations of child rights in Guatemala, emphasizing the need for immediate action to protect vulnerable populations.

## Advanced Science

### Physics
- **Quantum Mechanics:** Study of matter and energy at atomic scales.
- **Relativity:** Einstein's theory describing gravity as curvature of spacetime.
- **Schrödinger Equation:** Fundamental equation of quantum mechanics.

### Chemistry
- **Hybridization:** Mixing of atomic orbitals to form new hybrid orbitals.
- **Gibbs Free Energy (ΔG):** ΔG = ΔH - TΔS, predicts spontaneity of reactions.
- **NMR Spectroscopy:** Technique to determine molecular structure.

### Mathematics
- **Fourier Transform:** Decomposes functions into frequencies.
- **Eigenvalue:** Scalar associated with a linear system of equations.
- **Topology:** Study of properties preserved under continuous deformations.
